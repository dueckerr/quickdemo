The problem FIM is looking to solve is the lack of software designed for the OFS market. The Hydraulic Fracturing market in the United States alone is estimated at $6 billion. A market that large and volatile to maret prices, need to have software solutions dedicated to solve their specific problems, and FIM Solutions aims to do this. Indepented of the size the company, the industry standerd is to use excell spreadsheets, to track operational data.

Automation within OFS companies have laged behind. This has to do with the fact that little to no data is captured and shared from the field to the office. FIM's goal is to change this, first using the PIM Tracker to better manage pump components, such as Plungers, Valves, Packing, and Seats. These are the most costly parts associated with pump maintenance, averaging between $100,000 - $300,000 dependent on shale formations and job designs. This estimated cost does not include waste or other expense with regard to inventory management. This is the perverbial "low hanging fruit" FIM Solutions will work to remedy.
